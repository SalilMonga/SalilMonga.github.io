# Engineer’s Playground — Portfolio v2

## Vision
Engineer’s Playground is a personal portfolio designed as an immersive, intentional experience rather than a traditional résumé site.

The goal is to communicate:
- Curiosity-driven problem solving
- Systems thinking across software, XR, and physical engineering
- Craft, restraint, and luxury through interaction quality
- That meaningful work takes time, thought, and care

This is not a list of achievements.
This is a space to explore how I think.

---

## Core Principles

### 1. Experience Over Explanation
The site should *show* how I think before it tells.
Minimal copy, strong visuals, intentional motion.

### 2. Luxury Through Restraint
Not everything moves.
Not everything is interactive.
Anything that moves must have a reason.

### 3. Curiosity Is the Narrative
The site rewards exploration.
Interactions invite discovery rather than instruction.

### 4. Systems Thinking
Every section connects logically.
Software, XR, and mechanical interests are treated as different expressions of the same problem-solving mindset.

### 5. Desktop and Mobile Are First-Class
Desktop emphasizes exploration and depth.
Mobile emphasizes clarity, touch, and intimacy.
Mobile is not a watered-down version.

---

## High-Level Structure (Scroll Narrative)

### Level 1 — Entrance (Hero)
**Engineer’s Playground**

Purpose:
- Establish tone, taste, and confidence
- Create a sense of presence and depth
- Invite interaction without instruction

Characteristics:
- Neutral / charcoal base with a restrained accent color
- Large typography
- Soft glow and parallax
- Minimal copy

This section answers:
“Who is this person, and do they care about craft?”

---

### Level 2 — Workspace (Desk Environment)
**Primary SWE / Computer Science Identity**

Purpose:
- Anchor the portfolio in computer science and software engineering
- Show systems thinking and modern engineering context
- Establish credibility for SWE-focused roles

Environment:
- Dark, moody desk setup
- Multiple monitors
- Carefully chosen objects (not clutter)

Objects may include:
- Main monitor: systems, UI, architecture
- Secondary monitor: XR scene or garage preview
- VR headset
- Small car model
- Notebook / phone

Each object:
- Has subtle parallax
- Reveals a short story or principle on interaction
- Communicates curiosity and versatility

---

### Level 3 — Nested World: Garage
**Secondary Environment (Tinkerer / Builder Identity)**

Purpose:
- Show hands-on engineering mindset
- Connect physical systems to digital thinking
- Provide a memorable, signature moment

Narrative Framing:
- The garage exists *inside* the workspace
- Introduced as a widget, wallpaper, or screen
- Entered via zoom/focus transition

This avoids hard section breaks and reinforces systems thinking.

---

### Level 4 — XR / Simulation Layer
**Experimentation and Spatial Thinking**

Purpose:
- Showcase XR as a natural extension of engineering curiosity
- Avoid “demo reel” framing
- Present XR as a system being explored

Framing:
- A simulation running inside the garage
- A screen, hologram, or interactive overlay
- Invited, not forced

---

## Transitions Philosophy

- Transitions should feel like *focus shifts*, not page jumps
- Hero glow evolves into monitor backlight
- Desk transitions into garage through zoom and depth
- Everything feels connected and inevitable

If a transition feels flashy without meaning, it doesn’t ship.

---

## Interaction Language

- Soft physics (inertia, easing, weight)
- Parallax over gimmicks
- Discovery over instruction
- Ambient motion over constant motion

Motion exists to:
- Provide feedback
- Suggest depth
- Reinforce presence

---

## Timeline & Build Philosophy

This is a phased product build, not a sprint.

- v2 will live alongside v1 until ready
- Hero is built first and polished deeply
- Desk environment is second priority (SWE signal)
- Garage and XR layers can evolve over time

Target launch: Summer
Priority: Quality, cohesion, and confidence

---

## Success Criteria

The site succeeds if visitors feel:
- This person cares about craft
- This feels intentional and well thought out
- This engineer understands systems
- I want to talk to them

The site fails if it feels rushed, noisy, or performative.

---

## Reminder

When in doubt:
- Remove instead of add
- Slow down instead of speed up
- Trust restraint over spectacle
